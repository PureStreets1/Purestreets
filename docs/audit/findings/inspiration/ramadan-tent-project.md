## What it is
Ramadan Tent Project (UK, founded 2013 by students at SOAS) runs Open Iftar — the
UK's largest community iftar — at iconic venues (Trafalgar Square, Silverstone,
Banqueting House), welcoming "people of all faiths and none" to break fast,
eat and talk together. The nearest peer in spirit to PureStreets: Muslim-led,
student-born, community-first, faith expressed through service and welcome.
(Homepage observed directly via browser; scale/positioning corroborated by Hyphen.)

## What works
- **Type:** Restrained sans-serif with small letterspaced uppercase under a
  minimal geometric "tent" wordmark — calm and modern, not ornate.
- **Restraint:** A full-bleed deep teal/jade brand colour and a single white logo
  with generous negative space (the load screen is almost entirely empty) —
  confident quiet.
- **Colour:** One dominant jade-green identity; warm, dignified, distinctive.
- **Density:** Airy; long single-column scroll, one idea per band.
- **Motion:** Tasteful — a live Ramadan countdown and count-up impact stats
  ("People Connected", "Meals Distributed", "Volunteers") that animate from zero
  on scroll, the same family of behaviour PureStreets already uses.
- **Voice:** Hospitality-first — "Bringing communities together", "a home away
  from home", "turn strangers into friends"; faith shown through invitation, not
  preaching.

## One pattern to adapt
State an explicit **"everyone's welcome — all faiths and none" line high on the
page**. RTP's reach depends on signalling, up front, that the table is open
beyond the Muslim community. PureStreets says "everyone has a place here" lower
down; lifting an interfaith-welcome line near the hero tells a curious non-Muslim
neighbour, in one sentence, that the litter-pick is a community table they're
invited to — lowering the single biggest barrier to a wider turnout.
