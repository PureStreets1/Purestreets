## What it is
Incredible Edible (UK, started 2008 in Todmorden by Pam Warhurst, Mary Clear and
neighbours) is a grassroots movement growing free food on public land to build
kinder, more connected communities — now 120+ UK groups and 700+ worldwide. A
true bottom-up, replicable-anywhere model. (Live site verified; origin
corroborated by Wikipedia and Atlas of the Future.)

## What works
- **Type:** Sans-serif; bold large headlines for hierarchy, plain functional body
  — unfussy, reads as community-made not agency-polished.
- **Restraint:** White backgrounds, full-width banner images, breathing room
  around each content module so individual stories stand out.
- **Colour:** White + green (the growing theme) with warm earth tones supplied by
  photography — organic, optimistic, never corporate-sterile.
- **Density:** Airy, module-by-module; a carousel (Previous/Next) handles
  multiple community stories without crowding.
- **Photography:** Warm, candid, documentary shots of real members gardening and
  gathering — authenticity over polish does the persuading.
- **Voice:** Radically inclusive and disarming — "If you eat, you're in" and
  "An Incredible movement" make belonging the headline, not a footnote.

## One pattern to adapt
Distil the welcome into a **single, disarming one-line manifesto**. "If you eat,
you're in" instantly says *everyone belongs* and gives the movement a chant.
PureStreets already lists who's welcome (families, students, mosques,
neighbours); the move is to compress it into one punchy, repeatable inclusion
line used as a recurring rallying motto near the hero and in the footer — a motto
people can quote, not a paragraph they skim.
