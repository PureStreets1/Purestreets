## What it is
GoodGym (UK) is a community of people who "get fit by doing good": led group runs
that stop to do a practical task for the community — clearing a river of rubbish,
helping an older neighbour — then run home, all inside ~90 minutes. Service made
into a sociable, recurring local ritual. (Live site verified; model corroborated
by Nesta.)

## What works
- **Type:** Friendly sans-serif; headlines mostly sentence case ("Join a free
  workout that helps the environment and your community") so it reads like an
  invitation from a person, not an institution.
- **Restraint:** Lots of white space; full-width hero images alternate with text
  blocks so nothing competes — easy, scrollable, never cramped.
- **Colour:** Optimistic and clean — white-led with vibrant green/orange accents
  drawn from the action photography rather than imposed chrome.
- **Density:** Airy bands, generous spacing, one idea per block.
- **Motion:** Understated; the energy comes from imagery, not effects.
- **Photography:** Action shots of diverse, real participants mid-activity, plus
  authentic member headshots beside testimonials and a Guardian credibility nod.
  Tone: "feel great, and make a real change."

## One pattern to adapt
Present pickups as a **recurring, led, sociable group ritual with named local
groups**, not a flat events list. GoodGym wins because it pairs a habit (the run)
with the good deed and shows real faces saying it's welcoming. PureStreets could
frame each area as a named regular meet ("Cricklewood, every 2nd Saturday"), led
by an organiser, with a member quote — turning "an event" into "your group".
Builds on the existing events list; adds belonging and repeatability.
