## What it is
charity: water (US, founded 2006) funds clean-water projects in the developing
world and is the widely-cited gold standard for nonprofit design and trust: a
"100% model" (every public donation goes to the field) plus project maps and
impact reports showing exactly where money lands. (Live site verified; status as
design exemplar corroborated by multiple 2026 nonprofit-design roundups.)

## What works
- **Type:** Sans-serif; display treatment reserved for huge impact numerals
  (e.g. "21,641,908"), uppercase used sparingly for section labels/CTAs so
  hierarchy stays clean.
- **Restraint:** Distinctly airy — big white margins, generous gaps, content you
  scan and contemplate rather than fight through.
- **Colour:** Light grounds with teal/blue accents and warm earth tones; serious
  about the problem but hopeful, never guilt-driven despair.
- **Density:** Low; each block makes one point.
- **Photography:** Authentic documentary portraits (a child holding clean water)
  carry the emotion; illustration stays minimal and symbolic.
- **Trust as design:** Radical transparency is a *visual* feature — donation-
  methodology blocks, project maps, real-time-feeling counters. Voice: "Bring
  clean and safe water to every person on the planet", "clean water changes
  everything."

## One pattern to adapt
Make **transparency a visible design element**: show where the effort goes.
charity: water earns trust by mapping each funded project. PureStreets could give
each pickup a tiny public footprint — location, a before/after photo, the bags
lifted — so the site proves "your two hours produced *this visible change on
this street*". Concrete local proof is more persuasive than abstract totals, and
it reuses data the trackers already capture.
