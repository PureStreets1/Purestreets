## What it is
Surfers Against Sewage (UK, founded 1990 in Cornwall) is a marine-conservation
campaigning charity that mobilises volunteers for beach/river cleans and turns
the data into political pressure. Its "Million Mile Clean" recruits "hundreds of
thousands of Ocean Activists… to clean their local environment". (Homepage
observed directly via browser; design-system work corroborated by agency
ASquared.)

## What works
- **Type:** A rough, hand-painted/stencil display face for hero headlines
  ("Tell government to end the sewage scandal") gives protest-placard energy;
  body copy is a clean, calm sans for legibility — a deliberate loud/quiet pair.
- **Restraint via system:** Behind the punk surface sits a real design system —
  "a single source of truth… from colour, typography and spacing to form fields"
  (ASquared) — so the boldness stays consistent, not chaotic.
- **Colour:** Ocean blues/teals + a hot coral used only for action (CTAs read as
  hand-painted brush shapes, not rectangles), so the eye always finds "do this".
- **Density:** Airy hero; a torn/brush-stroke white reveal cuts the headline area
  out of a full-bleed photographic sea — texture as structure.
- **Motion/photography:** Real, slightly desaturated ocean photography under a
  painterly mask; restrained motion. Naming is participatory: a live sewage map,
  "Data HQ", and an activist identity for volunteers.

## One pattern to adapt
Give the movement a **named, recurring campaign with a collective target** —
SAS's "Million Mile Clean" turns thousands of tiny local acts into one shared
national number and gives each volunteer an identity ("Ocean Activist").
PureStreets could brand its pickups under one rolling banner with a visible
shared goal and a name for the people who show up, so an individual bin-bag feels
like joining something bigger. Pure framing/copy — no behaviour change.
