## What it is
The 2 Minute Foundation (UK national charity, grew out of the 2013
#2MinuteBeachClean hashtag) asks people to spend just two minutes picking litter
— beaches first, now streets, parks and woodland via #2MinuteLitterPick. Its
whole brand is built on lowering the barrier to act. (Live site verified;
campaign history corroborated by Ethos Magazine and Fjällräven.)

## What works
- **Type:** Sans-serif throughout; all-caps for the campaign hashtags
  (#2MINUTEBEACHCLEAN) as bold wordmark-like anchors, sentence-case body for
  readability. The hashtag *is* the logo.
- **Restraint:** A single, repeatable idea ("two minutes") carried across every
  page — one concept, stated plainly, not ten features.
- **Colour:** White ground with teal/turquoise + navy — ocean cues, optimistic
  and clean rather than alarmist.
- **Density:** Generous whitespace; the airy layout literally embodies "this is
  small and easy".
- **Motion/photography:** Lifestyle photography of ordinary people mid-pick plus
  simple sticker-style icons. Copy is conversational and disarming —
  "Have you got 2 minutes?" frames action as effortless, "little changes all add
  up" supplies the payoff.

## One pattern to adapt
Adopt a **radically low-barrier entry ask** as the primary CTA — reframe the
invitation from "volunteer" (sounds like commitment) to a tiny, finishable unit:
"Got 2 minutes? Fill one bag." A micro-action headline beside the hero lowers the
psychological threshold for a passer-by, a student or a family, then routes the
keen ones onward to a full pickup. It costs nothing but copy and placement.
