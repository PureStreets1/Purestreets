## What it is
Trash Free Trails (UK, founded 2017 by ex–Surfers Against Sewage organiser Dom
Ferris) is a grassroots non-profit that removes single-use pollution from trails
and wild places, run as a citizen-science movement: volunteers clean, then log
what they found. Closest peer to PureStreets — small org, litter as mission,
data as proof. (Live site verified; org corroborated by Sidetracked feature.)

## What works
- **Type:** One sans-serif family doing everything; confident uppercase nav and
  labels ("REPORT YOUR DATA", "GET INVOLVED") with display-weight headlines over
  lighter body. Hierarchy comes from weight + caps, not a second typeface.
- **Restraint:** Mostly white canvas, dark navy/charcoal ink, one job per band.
- **Colour:** Near-monochrome by design (white + ink) so the photography carries
  all the colour — a deliberate, disciplined choice.
- **Density:** Airy; the hero opens with negative space before content thickens
  toward CTAs and featured programmes.
- **Motion:** Quiet — collapsible nav sub-items, hover link states, a single
  running impact counter. No decorative animation.
- **Photography:** Saturated, action-led shots of real volunteers/riders
  mid-clean; genuine connection, never stock-sterile. Tone is hopeful not
  doom-laden — e.g. "We exist to protect our trails", and a counter,
  "removed and surveyed 572,830 individual items".

## One pattern to adapt
Promote a **single lifetime impact number to hero scale** as a confidence
device — one big, plain count ("X bags lifted", "Y streets cleaned") set in the
display face near the top, framed so every pick reads as a measured data point,
not just a chore. PureStreets already stores counts in localStorage; the move is
presentational — elevate one cumulative figure as upfront social proof instead
of leaving tallies buried lower down. Reuse the existing counter data; don't
change its behaviour.
