## What it is
A Rocha UK is a Christian environmental charity that does practical conservation
(nature reserves, habitat restoration) and equips churches to care for the
environment via Eco Church (8,000+ churches). The closest faith-rooted analogue
to PureStreets: a religious community turning belief into hands-on care for the
natural world. (Live site verified; Eco Church scale corroborated by org pages.)

## What works
- **Type:** Sans-serif throughout — modern, approachable, digital-first; no
  ecclesiastical heaviness.
- **Restraint:** Moderate density with real whitespace; prominent but calm
  navigation and "what we do" cards keep a broad remit legible.
- **Colour:** Earthy, nature-forward greens and natural tones — warmth and
  stewardship rather than institutional coldness.
- **Density:** Comfortable; a categorised noticeboard/news feed organises a lot of
  activity without clutter.
- **Photography:** Nature and wildlife (named species, landscapes) alongside real
  people doing conservation — belief shown through action, not stock piety.
- **Faith framing:** Faith is invitational and joyful, never guilt-driven —
  tagline "Caring for creation", and headlines like "Flourishing Nature,
  Flourishing People" tie spiritual purpose to tangible restoration.

## One pattern to adapt
Run a recurring **"why we do this" faith-as-invitation strip** that pairs a
teaching with a concrete action — A Rocha consistently frames stewardship as a
positive calling, not an obligation or a guilt trip. PureStreets already opens
with a hadith on removing harm from the path; the adaptable move is to make that
a *repeatable module* (short teaching + the practical act it implies, in a warm
invitational voice) rather than a one-off hero, so the faith thread runs through
the site as encouragement, never finger-wagging.
